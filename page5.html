Crop Recommendations for Sunny Weather, Loamy Soil, and High Irrigation

Corn

Description: Corn thrives in sunny weather and loamy soil. High irrigation ensures optimal growth.

Growth Period: 90-120 days

Yield: High

Special Notes: Requires regular watering and nitrogen-rich fertilizers.

Tomatoes

Description: Tomatoes grow well in sunny conditions and loamy soil. High irrigation supports fruit development.

Growth Period: 70-90 days

Yield: Moderate to High

Special Notes: Prune regularly to improve air circulation and prevent diseases.

Peppers

Description: Peppers prefer warm, sunny weather and well-drained loamy soil. High irrigation is beneficial.

Growth Period: 60-90 days

Yield: Moderate

Special Notes: Harvest when firm and fully colored for best flavor.

Soybeans

Description: Soybeans are well-suited for sunny weather and loamy soil. High irrigation ensures good pod formation.

Growth Period: 80-120 days

Yield: High

Special Notes: Fix nitrogen in the soil, making them great for crop rotation.

Cotton

Description: Cotton grows best in sunny, warm climates with loamy soil. High irrigation is essential for fiber development.

Growth Period: 150-180 days

Yield: High

Special Notes: Requires pest management due to susceptibility to bollworms.
